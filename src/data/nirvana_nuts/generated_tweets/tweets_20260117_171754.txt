1. You're not distracted because of your phone. You're distracted because you never decided what actually matters. Delete apps all you want—without clarity, you'll just find new ways to avoid the work.

2. Deep work isn't about blocking 4 hours. It's about the first 90 seconds after you sit down. Win that moment or lose the session. Your brain is testing whether you're serious.

3. Your to-do list is a graveyard of things that felt urgent but weren't important. Start each day by writing what you'd do if you only had 2 hours. Then do those first.
