## 1/

You don't fail at habits because you lack discipline.

You fail because you're trying to build the wrong ones.

Here's the system that actually works:

---

## 2/

Most people pick habits based on outcomes.

"I want to lose weight" → run 5 miles "I want to be rich" → wake up at 5am

Wrong approach.

> Pick habits based on identity first > Then design the smallest version possible

Ask: "What would a healthy person do today?" Not: "What gets me to my goal fastest?"

---

## 3/

The real killer: you're stacking too many changes.

Your brain treats each new habit like a threat. Add 3 at once? You're in survival mode.

The fix:

- One habit at a time - 2 minutes or less to start - Master it for 30 days before adding another

Boring beats ambitious every single time.

---

## 4/

Here's what nobody tells you:

> Habits fail in the gap between trigger and action

You need to eliminate that gap.

Put your running shoes by your bed. Pre-load your coffee maker the night before. Delete the apps that compete for your attention.

Make the right choice the automatic choice.

---

## 5/

The system is simple:

Identity → Micro-action → Remove friction

Not motivation. Not willpower. Just better design.

What's the ONE 2-minute habit you're starting tomorrow?
