1/ I stopped reading self-help books the day I realized:

Reading about change isn't changing.

It's procrastination with a PhD.

2/ Self-help creates the illusion of progress:

> You highlight the good parts > You nod along with insights > You feel like you're growing

Meanwhile, nothing in your actual life shifts.

3/ The brutal truth:

Most people collect self-help like trophies.

200 books on productivity. Still can't finish what matters.

Knowledge without action is just expensive entertainment.

4/ Here's what actually works:

Pick ONE thing you want to change. Do it for 30 days. Don't read about it. Don't optimize it. Just do it.

The doing teaches you more than any book ever will.

5/ Your life changes when your actions change.

Not when your bookshelf gets heavier.

Stop learning. Start doing.
