# The Protein Cult Will Give You Diabetes

Protein triggers insulin. Every gram you swallow spikes it. Yet bros on Instagram tell you to chug 200g daily while your grandparents thrived on dal and rice. The protein propaganda isn't just expensive—it's dangerous for Indian bodies.

## The Insulin Truth They Hide

**Protein isn't metabolically innocent.** It triggers insulin release through amino acids, especially leucine. For a population where 1 in 6 Indians has diabetes and 15% more are pre-diabetic, this matters brutally.

Your body doesn't care that protein is "clean." It responds. Studies show high-protein diets increase insulin demand by 20-30%. When you're genetically wired for insulin resistance—like most South Asians—you're playing with fire.

**The dose makes the poison.** 0.8g per kg bodyweight is enough for most people. Anything beyond 1.2g without serious athletic training is excess your body must process. That processing costs you metabolic health.

## You're Pricing Out the Poor

Eggs hit ₹8-10 each in metros. Milk prices jumped 40% in two years. Paneer costs more than chicken in some markets.

**Your protein obsession creates scarcity.** When the middle class hoards protein based on imported fitness trends, the poor—who actually need it—can't afford basic nutrition. A laborer's child needs those eggs more than your fourth meal-prep container.

The irony? **India has protein deficiency among the poor and protein overconsumption among the privileged.** Both groups suffer. One from lack, one from excess that their insulin-resistant genes can't handle.

## Your Ancestors Weren't Stupid

Dal-chawal kept generations alive through famines. Idli-sambar built temple sculptors who worked 12-hour days. Ragi mudde powered farmers who plowed fields manually.

**They ate 40-60g of protein daily and didn't collapse.** They got fiber, resistant starch, polyphenols, and minerals that actually prevent diabetes. Their meals had 10-15g of fiber per serving. Yours has 2g and 40g of protein.

Coconut oil was medicine. Ghee was sacred. Millets were staples. Then colonized minds decided everything brown and traditional was inferior. Now you're paying ₹400 for quinoa that does less than ₹40 of ragi.

**The Western protein obsession comes from a different gene pool.** Europeans and Americans have different insulin responses, different gut bacteria, different dietary history. Their advice isn't universal truth—it's cultural export.

## What Actually Works

Moderation isn't sexy, but it saves lives.

**Calculate your actual need:** Bodyweight in kg × 0.8 to 1g. A 70kg person needs 56-70g protein daily. Not 150g. Not six eggs plus three scoops of powder.

**Cut refined carbs first.** Maida, white rice, sugar—these are the real villains. They spike insulin faster and harder than protein ever will. Replace them with millets, whole grains, and vegetables.

**Load up on fiber.** 30-40g daily from vegetables, legumes, and whole grains. Fiber slows digestion, reduces insulin spikes, feeds good gut bacteria. It's the nutrient nobody sells you because it's too cheap.

**Eat more vegetables than everything else combined.** Half your plate. Every meal. Non-negotiable. They provide volume, nutrients, and protection without insulin chaos.

**Question every fad.** When someone tells you to abandon traditional foods, ask: Who benefits? Usually, it's someone selling supplements, courses, or imported foods.

## The Real Enemy

**Processed food companies want you confused.** When you're scared of rice and ghee, you buy their protein bars and shakes. When you think traditional food is inadequate, you become a customer for life.

The protein propaganda isn't about your health. It's about market creation. India's fitness industry is projected to hit $32 billion. Every confused person is revenue.

**Your body already knows what it needs.** Hunger, satiety, energy levels—these are signals. The problem? You've been taught to ignore them and follow influencers who get paid per post.

Diabetes doesn't care about your gains. Insulin resistance doesn't reverse because your biceps grew. **Metabolic health trumps muscle mass every single time.**
